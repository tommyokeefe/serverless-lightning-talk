Serverless Architecture

in 10 minutes or less

Basic look at what people mean by "Serverless" What it is, and why it may or may not be right for your project.

Alexa, tell me about serverless architecture

AWS Lambda is the recommended way to do Alexa development. I wasn't content with just doing it, I wanted to understand how it worked and why I should use it. That led me on a deep dive that I'll give you a few highlights from.

Serverless Architecture:

What is it?

  • Backend as a service (BaaS)
  • Functions as a service (FaaS)
    • Backend: not talking about that today. Firebase is a good example. Functions: Our focus. Tere is a growing number of frameworks and libraries, one is even called serverless. We won't be talking about those today either.

FaaS === a logical evolution of Platform as a Service (PaaS)

  • PaaS: long running containers that provide ease of use & scalability
  • PaaS examples: Heroku, BitNami, Google AppEngine, etc..
  • FaaS examples: AWS Lambda (Amazon), Azure Functions (Microsoft), Cloud Functions (Google), OpenFaaS & Fn Project.

FaaS === ephemeral containers

transient stateless containers that scale

  • Containers: Run your code in here, without worrying about hardware and OS.
  • Transient: They're here, then *poof*, they're gone!
  • Stateless: You need state? Put it in a database.
  • Scale: don't worry about capacity, we'll scale it automatically to meet demand.

Is it right for me?
Maybe.
questions like "Why should I use it? When should I use it?" presume that you should use this. Maybe you should, maybe you shouldn't. I don't know if you should, but I can share what I see as some pros and cons.

FaaS Pros:

  • Cost effective
  • Can scale automagically
  • Deployments can be simple and straight-forward
  • Removes a lot of (unnessecary?) overhead
  • Great for microservices & event-driven apps

FaaS Cons:

  • #NoOps is a lie
  • The learning curve is steep
  • Coldstarts can be a problem
  • Not efficient for long running tasks
  • Testing & running locally can be a challenge
  • Vendor lock-in is real

If you remember anything:

  1. Call it FaaS
  2. It probably won't change your life
  3. It probably won't ruin your life
  4. It might be a good option
Numbers 2 and 3 are probably applicable to any "new" thing in the tech world.
